# Image generation prompts

Both assets were generated with the built-in `image_gen` tool. The original Curved World concept screen was used only as a character and art-direction reference.

## `professor-bent-classroom.png`

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: in-app tutor video feed illustration for a SvelteKit web app
Input images: Image 1 is a strict character and art-direction reference only. Preserve Professor Bent’s recognizable identity from the right-hand video panel: teal-blue skin, elongated crescent-curved head, large amber eyes, round brass spectacles, swept white side hair and eyebrows, burgundy academic robe with gold piping, mint bow tie, friendly eccentric expression.
Primary request: Create a clean standalone landscape scene of Professor Bent teaching from his hyperbolic geometry study, intended to fill a video-call panel. He faces the viewer and raises one index finger as if explaining a surprising theorem. Frame him from mid-torso upward.
Scene/backdrop: cozy dark-indigo academic observatory, chalkboard behind him with subtle curved geodesic diagrams, blurred books, a small glowing brass globe and warm amber lamp. The architecture and chalk lines feel gently curved by non-Euclidean space.
Style/medium: polished whimsical editorial character illustration with softly dimensional 3D-like painted rendering, matching Image 1; crisp enough for a production UI asset.
Composition/framing: landscape 16:9, professor centered slightly right, head and hands fully inside frame, enough background around him for responsive cropping.
Lighting/mood: warm amber rim light against cool teal and indigo, clever, welcoming, mildly uncanny.
Color palette: teal skin, deep navy background, burgundy robe, mint bow tie, amber brass highlights.
Constraints: no interface chrome, no speech bubble, no captions, no readable text, no logos, no watermark. One character only. Preserve the exact costume and species cues from Image 1.
Avoid: UI elements, geometry labels, photorealism, extra people, cropped head, cropped hands, scary expression, generic human professor.
```

## `hyperbolic-whiteboard.png`

```text
Use case: scientific-educational
Asset type: flat 2D whiteboard image used inside a web app
Input images: Image 1 is a visual reference for the central hyperbolic geometry board only. Do not include the professor, tutor panel, sidebar, header, buttons, or any surrounding app interface.
Primary request: Create a clean standalone 2D illustration of a Poincaré disk geometry lesson. Show a large circular dark-navy hyperbolic plane with a thin warm-ivory rim, delicate cyan geodesic grid arcs near the boundary, and one prominent triangle made of three warm-ivory curved geodesics. Add three colored angle wedges: coral at the top, mint at lower left, amber at lower right.
Style/medium: flat vector-like scientific classroom illustration with subtle paper grain and luminous ink; strictly 2D, no character, no room, no three-dimensional objects.
Composition/framing: square 1:1 image, straight-on, entire circular disk visible with generous dark-navy padding; the triangle is large and centered.
Color palette: deep navy, warm ivory, cyan, coral, mint, amber.
Text (verbatim): “60°”, “42°”, “41°”, “ANGLE SUM 143°”
Typography: clear compact geometric sans-serif, large enough to read in an app panel. Render each exact label once.
Constraints: The whiteboard must contain geometry only. No people, no creatures, no faces, no professor, no video frame, no UI controls, no title bar, no speech bubble, no logos, no watermark. Keep the disk mathematically suggestive and visually legible.
Avoid: 3D rendering, classroom scene, human or alien character, ornate interface chrome, extra text, perspective view, photorealism.
```
