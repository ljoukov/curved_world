# Three-eyed Professor Bent image-generation prompts

Both sprite sheets used the accepted three-eyed Professor Bent portrait as a
strict identity, anatomy, costume, palette, and rendering reference.

## Talking sheet

```text
Create exactly eight consecutive talking frames in a precise 4-column by
2-row grid. Keep the same three-eyed Professor Bent, with exactly three real
amber eyes and three connected antique-gold spectacle lenses. Lock all three
pupil centers, head position, scale, rotation, gaze, eyebrows, glasses, hair,
shoulders, costume, lighting, camera, and crop. All three eyes stay fully open.

Frame order: relaxed closed mouth; small open mouth; A/AH; E/EE; rounded O;
narrow U/OO; F/V with upper teeth touching the lower lip; nearly closed return.
Change only the mouth and lower jaw.

Use a perfectly flat uniform #ff00ff chroma-key background in every cell, with
no shadows, gradients, texture, floor, halo, vignette, reflections, scenery,
labels, borders, or dividers. Do not use #ff00ff on the character. No hands,
props, text, logos, or watermark. This is a 2D raster sprite asset, not a 3D
mesh deliverable.
```

## Expression sheet

```text
Create exactly eight distinct emotional portraits in a precise 4-column by
2-row grid. Keep the same three-eyed Professor Bent, with exactly three real
amber eyes and three connected antique-gold spectacle lenses. Natural changes
to eyebrows, eyelids, pupils, mouth, and subtle head angle are welcome.

Expression order: neutral attentive; warm happy; delighted; surprised;
concerned; skeptical; thinking with all pupils looking upward; reassuring.

Use the same chest-up framing and broadly consistent scale in every cell. Use
a perfectly flat uniform #ff00ff chroma-key background with no shadows,
gradients, texture, floor, halo, vignette, reflections, scenery, labels,
borders, or dividers. Do not use #ff00ff on the character. No hands, props,
text, logos, or watermark. Exactly three eyes and three spectacle lenses in
every frame.
```
